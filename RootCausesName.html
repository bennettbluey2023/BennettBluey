Root Causes

Root Cause #1

A high number of athletes are scrutinized by teammates, media, and fans for reaching out for help or not living up to expectations.

Root Cause #2

Athletes are unsure how and who to talk to/about their mental health issues.

Evidence: 1) Approximately 30% of women and 25% of men who are student-athletes report having anxiety

2) Limited privacy, inadequate recovery time and limited control or independence may also add to the stress of a professional or elite athlete.

Evidence: 1) Professional and elite athletes also face mental health challenges, and data indicate that approximately 35% of elite athletes suffer from disordered eating, burnout, depression and/or anxiety

2) Only 10% of all college athletes with known mental health conditions seek care from a mental health professional.

  • Explanation - These numbers are just student athletes who report having anxiety, there are countless more who stay silent out of fear of getting scrutinized and labeled as weak. Explanation - Athletes are held to higher expectations than other people, and are expected to perform at a high level on the field while maintaining off the field responsibilities.
  • Explanation - 1 in every 3 pro athletes face these severe mental health issues which shows how serious and common this issue is within the athletic field. Explanation - Athletes are not comfortable with reaching out for help, meaning the issue never stops, it continues spiraling out of control.
